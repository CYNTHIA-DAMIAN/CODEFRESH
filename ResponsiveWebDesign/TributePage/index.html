Dr. Nnamdi Azikiwe, popularly known as “Zik of Africa” or “Zik”, was Governor General of Nigeria from 1960 to 1963 and the first president of Nigeria during the First Nigerian Republic (1963–1966).

Here's a timeline of Dr. Nnamdi Azikiwe's life:

  • 1904 - Born in Nigeria
  • 1934 - Athletic Career: Nnamdi founded Zik's Athletic Club (ZAC) which would open its doors to sportsmen and women of all races, nationalities, tribes, and classes of Nigeria.
  • 1930- Writing Career: Azikiwe, hearing of the unruly event, wrote an article of condemnation titled ‘Murdering women in Nigeria’ while still studying in the United States in 1930 and a letter which he addressed to the then Pan-Africanist civil rights activist, WEB du bois.
  • 1940- Cpnspiracy Allegations: As a result of Azikiwe's support for a general strike in June 1945 and his attacks on the colonial government, publication of the West African Pilot was suspended on 8 July of that year.
  • 1946- Zikist Movement: The youth rallied to fight against the colonial autocracy of Great Britain. One such group of young people were the Zikists.
  • 1945- Opposition to Richards constitution: the changes were opposed by nationalists such as Azikiwe.
  • 1950-1953- Legislative Council: Under the Richards constitution, Azikiwe was elected to the Legislative Council in a Lagos municipal election from the National Democratic Party (an NCNC subsidiary).
  • 1954- Premier of Eastern region: Azikiwe became the premier of the Eastern Region in 1954 after a new constitution was put into effect
  • 1960- Nigeria Independence: Azikiwe was elected president of Nigeria in 1960 and declared independence from the British Empire.
  • 1960- Governor General of Nigeria: zikiwe was recommended as the successor to Sir James Robertson, by the British Prime Minister Harold Macmillan, during his visit to Lagos in early 1960
  • 1963-1966- Presidency: The Federal Republic of Nigeria was established on 1 October 1963, with Abubakar Tafawa Balewa as prime minister (head of government) and Benjamin Nnamdi Azikiwe as president (head of state) and commander in chief of Armed forces.
  • 1965-1996- Post presidency and final years: Azikiwe eventually died on 11 May 1996, at the University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital in Enugu after a prolonged illness
  • 1960-1963- Historical reputation and legacy: Viceregal styles of Nnamdi Azikiwe's clothing, his political views, and his support for the Zikist movement were well-known and respected in Nigeria.
  • 1960- Honors: Member of the Privy Council of the United Kingdom, 16 November 1960. Grand Commander of the Order of the Federal Republic (GCFR).
  • 1946-1980- Honorary degrees: Dr Azikiwe was awarded 14 honorary degrees from Nigerian, American and Liberian universities
  • 2001- Memorials, monuments, Currency and postage stamps:His image was included in the postage stamps unveiled during the first anniversary of Federal Republic of Nigeria in 1964 and has since appeared in other stamps.
  • 1970- In popular culture: The first biography of Azikiwe was authored by himself, published in 1970, titled My Odyssey: An Autobiography
  • 1946- Traditional honor: Azikiwe was inducted into the Agbalanze society of Onitsha as Nnanyelugo in 1946, a recognition for Onitsha men with significant accomplishments.
  • 1973- Political ideologies: Zikism; it is the most popular political ideology of Nnamdi Azikiwe, aimed at decolonizing the minds of young Africans.
  • 1931- Works: Azikiwe's works include The Nnamdi Azikiwe Collection, which includes his autobiography, The Zik of Africa, and The Zik of Nigeria.
